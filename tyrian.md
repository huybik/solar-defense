# Tyrian 2000 — Full Breakdown

## Overview
Developed by Eclipse Software (Jason Emery), published by Epic MegaGames. Originally Tyrian (1995), then Tyrian 2000 (1999) with a bonus 5th episode. Vertical scrolling shooter, DOS era, later released free. Written in Turbo Pascal of all things.

## Game Modes

**Full Game (Story)** — 5 episodes, ~40 levels total. Between missions you shop for weapons/upgrades. Story told through data terminals and briefings. You play as Trent Hawkins, a terraforming pilot who witnesses a murder and gets pulled into a corporate war over a mineral called Gravitium.

**Arcade** — Classic pick-up-and-play. No shop. Grab weapons from drops mid-level. 2-player co-op (one flies, one controls a floating turret — very unique split).

**Super Tyrian / Destruct** — Hidden bonus modes. Destruct is a Scorched Earth artillery mini-game. Super Tyrian is a harder remix.

## The Map / Level Structure

- **Vertical scrolling**, always moving upward
- Levels vary in environment: space, planetary surfaces, asteroid fields, industrial stations, organic alien zones
- Backgrounds are multi-layered parallax — stars, nebulae, planet surfaces, mechanical structures
- Some levels have branching paths — you choose which mission to take next on an episode map screen
- Terrain is mostly cosmetic but some levels have destructible ground structures (turrets, buildings) you fly over
- Levels are ~3-5 minutes each, ending in a boss or a "clear the wave" finale
- Between levels: a top-down sector map showing your route through the episode, sometimes with optional side paths

## Ship Types

You don't pick a ship class per se in Story mode — you upgrade ONE ship progressively. But the ship changes visually as you upgrade the hull.

**Ships / Generators (Story mode):**
- **USP Talon** — starter ship, weak armor
- **Gencore Phoenix** — mid-tier
- **Gencore Maelstrom** — heavy
- **MicroCorp Stalker** — fast, less armor
- **Stormwind** — balanced endgame ship
- Several others unlockable

**Generator** determines your energy regeneration rate (powers special weapons). Upgrading generator = more frequent special weapon use.

**Shields** — separate purchase. Determines how many hits you absorb. Some shields regenerate slowly.

**Armor** — your hull. When shields are gone, armor takes hits. Armor doesn't regenerate.

## Weapon System (The Heart of Tyrian)

This is where Tyrian shines. Five weapon slots:

1. **Front Gun** — your primary. Fires forward. ~30+ options:
   - Pulse Cannon — balanced rapid fire
   - Multi-Cannon — spread shot
   - Protron series — energy bolts, various patterns
   - Vulcan Cannon — fast bullet stream
   - Laser — piercing beam
   - SDF Main Gun — heavy damage, slow rate
   - Mega Cannon — wide devastation
   - Zica Supercharger — charges up, releases burst
   - Each gun has ~11 power levels. You buy upgrades incrementally.

2. **Rear Gun** — fires backward or in special patterns:
   - Starburst — fires in all directions
   - Multi-Cannon Rear — backward spread
   - Sonic Impulse — rear wave
   - Banana Blast — yes, it shoots bananas (Tyrian has humor)
   - Protron Dispersal — rear scatter
   - Some rear guns fire sideways or create orbiting projectiles

3. **Left Sidekick** — autonomous drone on your left:
   - Options include: extra guns, missiles, plasma storm, companion fighters
   - Some track enemies, some fire in fixed directions

4. **Right Sidekick** — same system, right side. You can mix and match different sidekicks.

5. **Special Weapon** — uses energy (from generator):
   - Missiles (homing, MIRV, guided)
   - MegaBombs — screen-clearing nuke
   - Plasma Storm
   - Attractor — pulls pickups toward you
   - Repulsor — pushes away bullets
   - SandStorm — area denial
   - Ice Beam, Flare, etc.
   - You can carry multiple types, switch mid-level

## Power-Up / Pickup System

In **Arcade mode**, enemies drop colored orbs:
- Weapon powerups change/upgrade your current gun
- Purple orbs — special weapon ammo
- Shields/armor repair pickups

In **Story mode**, upgrades are bought between levels at the shop using credits earned from kills and collecting floating money pickups during levels. The shop has a full inventory screen — very RPG-like.

**Data Cubes** — hidden in destructible terrain. Unlock lore entries and sometimes secret levels.

## Enemy Types

Massive variety across 5 episodes:

- **Small fighters** — basic, fly in formations, fire single shots
- **Dive bombers** — swoop toward you kamikaze-style
- **Turrets** — stationary ground targets, various firing patterns (aimed, spread, ring)
- **Heavy ships** — take multiple hits, fire burst patterns
- **Shielded enemies** — require sustained fire to break through
- **Organic enemies** (later episodes) — alien creatures, erratic movement
- **Drone spawners** — large ships that release smaller fighters
- **Mine layers** — drop floating mines in your path
- **Beam ships** — charge and fire a laser line you have to dodge
- **Cloaked enemies** — shimmer in and out of visibility

Enemies arrive in scripted waves — formations fly in from top, sides, sometimes behind you. Patterns repeat but mix in unexpected ways.

## Boss Fights

Every episode has multi-stage bosses:

- **Bosses are LARGE** — fill significant screen space
- Multi-part: you destroy individual turrets, engines, weapon pods. The boss degrades visually as you damage it.
- Attack patterns shift per phase — first phase might be aimed shots, second adds bullet rings, third adds homing missiles
- Some bosses scroll with you (flying alongside), others are stationary structures you fly past
- A few memorable ones:
  - Giant mechanical centipede — segments break off
  - Space stations with rotating gun arrays
  - Organic alien motherships that split apart
  - Episode 5 (Tyrian 2000 bonus) has particularly wild bosses

## Scoring & Economy

- **Credits** — earned per kill, scale with difficulty. Also floating dollar-sign pickups in levels.
- Between missions, spend credits at the shop on weapons, ship parts, shields, generators
- Strategic choice: do you max one front gun or diversify? Upgrade shields or buy a better generator?
- Score displayed on completion, but the economy is the real progression hook

## Difficulty

5 difficulty levels:
- Easy / Normal / Hard / Impossible / Suicide (unlockable)
- Affects enemy bullet density, health, and how many credits you earn
- Higher difficulty = more money = can afford better gear faster, but you die more

## Secret / Hidden Content

Tyrian is STUFFED with secrets:
- Hidden levels accessible by destroying specific objects
- Secret shops with unique weapons
- Destruct mini-game (artillery mode)
- Super Tyrian mode — remixed harder campaign
- Easter eggs throughout (developer messages, joke weapons)
- The banana weapon is real and usable
- "Pretzel" collectibles in certain levels

## The Feel

- **Speed** — scrolling is moderate but gameplay is fast. Your ship is nimble.
- **Bullet density** — not a true bullet hell, but Hard+ difficulty gets dense
- **Weight** — weapons feel chunky. Big guns have visible recoil on your ship sprite, screen fills with explosions.
- **Music** — Alexander Brandon & Andreas Molnar composed the soundtrack. FM synth, memorable tracks. Considered one of the best DOS game soundtracks.
- **Humor** — the game doesn't take itself too seriously. Banana weapons, silly data log entries, a secret food-themed level.

## Why It's Special

Most shmups are pure reflex. Tyrian added an **RPG progression layer** — the shop between levels turns it into a build-crafting game. You're constantly thinking about loadout optimization, not just dodging. That combination was unique in 1995 and still holds up.
